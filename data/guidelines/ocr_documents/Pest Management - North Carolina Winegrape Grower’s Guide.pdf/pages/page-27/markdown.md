Delaying the PRE herbicide for 6 to 8 weeks extends PRE weed control for 6 to 8 weeks in the summer. The March glyphosate application provides control into early May. Therefore, there is no benefit to applying a PRE herbicide prior to that time.

☐ Fall/Spring Split Program. Grape growers in areas where weeds germinate throughout the winter and summer should consider this

program (piedmont). This program begins with a fall PRE application in combination with a nonselective burndown herbicide like paraquat or glufosinate applied after harvest. In late spring a PRE herbicide with glyphosate should be applied for residual summer annual weed control when control from the fall application fails. When a fall PRE herbicide is applied post-harvest, the spring PRE herbicide is applied in late May in western North Carolina.
